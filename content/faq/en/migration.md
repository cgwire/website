## Switching Without Slowing Down Production

Every studio hesitates before changing its production tracker. That hesitation is justified: you have deadlines, artists in the middle of shots, and producers relying on stable data every day.

A poorly handled migration can create confusion and cost real money.

Kitsu is built with that reality in mind. 

The goal is not to force a sudden change, but to let your studio transition at its own pace while production continues. You can move one project at a time, or even one department at a time, without interrupting deliveries. No downtime, no production freeze.

## A Migration Plan That Matches How Studios Actually Work

You are not expected to figure everything out alone. Migration is handled as a structured collaboration between your team and CGWire.

The process is straightforward and grounded in real production workflows:

* Review your current setup including projects, task structures, and roles
* Translate your pipeline into Kitsu without forcing unnecessary changes
* Import your data and verify that everything is usable
* Train production leads first so they can guide their teams
* Roll out to artists with clear and simple task views
* Adjust based on real usage, not assumptions

For most studios, this takes days to a few weeks depending on complexity. The key point is that it stays predictable.

## Keeping Your Data Intact and Usable

Your production data is not just files. It is your history, your decisions, your communication.

Kitsu allows you to import structured data through CSV or API, so you can bring over:

* Projects, sequences, episodes
* Assets and shots with their versions
* Task structures and assignments
* Status workflows
* Comments and production notes

If you are coming from another tool, CGWire helps you extract and reorganize that data so it fits cleanly into Kitsu.

## Running Both Systems If You Need Reassurance

Some studios prefer to validate everything before committing. That is a reasonable approach.

You can run your existing system alongside Kitsu for a short period. During that time, your producers can compare progress reports, task updates, artist assignments, and time tracking data.

This gives your team confidence that nothing is missing or misaligned. Once everything matches expectations, switching fully becomes an easy decision rather than a leap of faith.

## Training That Respects Each Role

Adoption usually fails when tools feel disconnected from daily work.

Kitsu avoids that by focusing on how different roles actually operate:

* Artists get clear task lists and simple update workflows
* Supervisors focus on reviews, annotations, and approvals
* Producers track schedules, quotas, and budgets

Training is adapted to each role with live sessions, practical examples, and direct support during rollout. We make sure each person understands how Kitsu helps them do their job faster.

## Integration Without Rebuilding Your Pipeline

Most studios already have a working pipeline. The concern is whether a new tool will force a rebuild.

Kitsu is designed to integrate, not replace.

With its REST API and Python client, your technical team can connect it to:

* DCC tools for publishing previews directly
* Asset pipelines for production management
* Render farm systems for tracking outputs
* Internal asset systems if you already have one
* Review workflows already used by supervisors

An artist can publish a playblast from their DCC tool and have it automatically appear in Kitsu with the correct task and version without manual upload.

## Open Source Means Control, Not Dependency

Switching tools should reduce risk, not create a new dependency.

Kitsu being open source gives you options:

* You can host it in the cloud or on your own infrastructure
* You can access and audit your data at any time
* You can adapt it if your pipeline evolves

This is particularly important for studios working with clients who require strict data control or specific infrastructure setups.

With Kitsu, you can move progressively, keep production running, preserve your data, and we keep on supporting your team at every step.
